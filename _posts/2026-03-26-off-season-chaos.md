The offseason brought even more change.

**Rahul Dravid stepped down as head coach** after a disappointing 2025 campaign. While many fans were saddened by his departure, there was also a feeling that a fresh start was needed. Under **Kumar Sangakkara**, **Rajasthan Royals had built some of their strongest squads and most successful campaigns**, making his return to the top coaching role a move that many quietly welcomed.

But the biggest shock was yet to come.

For over a decade, **Sanju Samson wasn't just Rajasthan Royals' captain; he was the face of the franchise**. Through highs and lows, playoff runs and disappointing finishes, Sanju had become synonymous with Rajasthan Royals. So when reports emerged that he would be leaving the franchise, it felt almost impossible to imagine.

Eventually, after extensive discussions, **Samson was traded to Chennai Super Kings**. While it was undoubtedly an **emotional moment for RR fans**, it was a decision that deserved respect. **Some players become bigger than franchises**, and Sanju had earned the right to make the choice he felt was best for his career.

In return, **Rajasthan received Sam Curran and Ravindra Jadeja**.

From a purely cricketing perspective, the trade **addressed two of RR's biggest weaknesses from 2025**. **Jadeja immediately strengthened a spin attack** that had lacked control and wicket-taking ability, while **Curran brought something every T20 side covets**: a genuine overseas fast-bowling all-rounder capable of contributing in every phase of the game.

The Royals weren't done yet.

To **add more firepower to the lower middle order, they acquired Donovan Ferreira** from Delhi Capitals in exchange for Nitish Rana. Ferreira's ability to clear boundaries from ball one gave RR an attacking option they had often lacked when rebuilding innings wasn't an option.

With the major trades completed, the shape of the squad started becoming clear.

**Yashasvi Jaiswal and Vaibhav Sooryavanshi formed an exciting opening pair**. **Riyan Parag would continue as the centerpiece of the middle order**. **Hetmyer, Jurel and Ferreira provided finishing power**, while **Jadeja and Curran added genuine batting depth** without weakening the bowling attack.

For the first time in years, **Rajasthan Royals looked like a side that could comfortably bat all the way down to number eight or even nine**. The batting wasn't just strong at the top anymore—it was **deep, flexible and built for modern T20 cricket**.

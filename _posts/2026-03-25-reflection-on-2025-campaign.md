After finishing **9th in IPL 2025** with just **four wins in fourteen matches**, Rajasthan Royals entered the 2026 season under immense pressure. Questions surrounded almost every decision the franchise had made over the previous year.

The biggest criticism came during the retention cycle. RR chose to back a young core of **Dhruv Jurel, Riyan Parag and Shimron Hetmyer** while **letting go** of proven match-winners like **Jos Buttler**. Many fans and experts felt the franchise had prioritized potential over certainty, especially after Buttler's consistent performances for the Royals over several seasons. Former players and analysts openly questioned whether RR had made a **massive auction mistake**.

The concerns only grew as the season unfolded. The **middle order repeatedly failed to close out games**, with **Hetmyer, Jurel and Shubham Dubey** struggling to finish chases and convert strong positions into victories. **RR lost several close matches** despite getting themselves into winning situations, exposing a **lack of composure in crunch moments**.

Their bowling attack faced equal scrutiny. The **spin department lacked penetration and control**, while overseas spinners failed to provide the impact the management had hoped for. The pace attack wasn't much better. Although **Jofra Archer showed flashes of brilliance**, **RR regularly leaked runs at an alarming rate**, most notably **conceding 286 against Sunrisers Hyderabad** early in the season. By the end of 2025, there was a growing belief that Rajasthan Royals had **lost the balance** that once made them one of the smartest teams in the league.

Heading into 2026, expectations were arguably lower than they had been in years. For many observers, **RR weren't viewed as playoff contenders**. They were viewed as a franchise trying to justify the decisions that had led them to one of their worst seasons in recent memory.

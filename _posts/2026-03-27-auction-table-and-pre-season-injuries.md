With the major trades completed, attention shifted to the **auction table**.

The requirements were fairly straightforward. Rajasthan Royals needed **another quality spinner to partner Ravindra Jadeja**, some mystery in the bowling attack, and **batting depth** to ensure that injuries or poor form wouldn't derail the season.

Most importantly, the management needed to prove that they had **learned from the mistakes of 2025**.

The auction reflected exactly that.

**RR secured Ravi Bishnoi**, one of the premier Indian T20 spinners, immediately strengthening a spin department that had looked toothless the previous season. To **add variety and unpredictability, they brought in Yash Raj and Vignesh Puthur**, giving the bowling attack options that could trouble batters unfamiliar with their styles.

The pace-bowling reserves were also addressed. **Kuldeep Sen returned to the franchise** in a homecoming of sorts, while **Adam Milne provided experienced overseas cover** in case injuries struck the fast-bowling group.

The batting additions were less glamorous but equally important. **Aman Rao and Ravi Singh added depth** and ensured that RR had backup options without disrupting the balance of the first-choice XI.

Unlike some auctions where teams chase big names and headlines, **Rajasthan's approach felt methodical**. Every signing appeared to solve a specific problem that had been exposed during the previous campaign.

The strategy was clear.

**Identify weaknesses. Fix them. Move on.**

And by the end of the auction, it was difficult to argue that RR hadn't achieved exactly what they had set out to do.

Then came the **first major setback**.

**Sam Curran suffered an injury before the season began.**

At first glance it looked like the loss of just another overseas player. In reality, it was much bigger than that.

**Curran was arguably the glue that held the squad together.** With the bat, he offered tremendous flexibility. He could stabilize an innings, accelerate against spin, or finish games depending on the situation. With the ball, he provided valuable new-ball swing, **formed a natural partnership with Jofra Archer**, and could contribute useful overs through the middle phase.

Very few players offer that combination of skills.

**Losing Curran before a ball had even been bowled wasn't just losing a player—it was losing balance.** Suddenly, many of the carefully constructed plans RR had built during the offseason needed rethinking.

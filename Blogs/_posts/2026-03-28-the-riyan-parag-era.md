The final major decision before the season began was perhaps the **most important one of all**.

**Who would lead this new-look Rajasthan Royals side?**

With **Kumar Sangakkara back in charge**, the franchise had an opportunity to redefine its identity. The squad had changed. The coaching structure had changed. Even the face of the franchise had changed. The next captain would play a huge role in determining whether **RR's rebuild succeeded or failed**.

Rather than making a quick appointment, the management adopted a **structured and methodical approach**. From a **shortlist of five candidates**—ranging from young players like **Yashasvi Jaiswal and Dhruv Jurel** to veterans like **Ravindra Jadeja and Sandeep Sharma**—Sangakkara and the management ran **formal, one-on-one interviews** built around **deeply targeted and uncomfortable questions** on tactics and match scenarios, grading each on **critical thinking, authenticity and self-reflection** and the difference between **"captaining" and "leading"**.

The decision wasn't about finding the most experienced player.

It was about **finding the right leader for the future**.

And eventually, **the choice was Riyan Parag**.

**Parag had set himself apart with a distinct, long-term vision** and a desire to actively lead team culture rather than just manage on-field changes. After discussions to ensure **absolute alignment**, the final call was made by **Badale and Sangakkara**, who notified him of his appointment **via a phone call**.

The appointment **divided opinion**. Some saw a young player with immense confidence and leadership potential. Others questioned whether he had enough experience to captain a side carrying playoff ambitions.

But those who had followed Parag's journey closely knew exactly why RR had backed him.

**He was fearless.**

Parag's approach to cricket had always been **aggressive**, whether with the bat, in the field or in the way he carried himself. He wasn't afraid of responsibility, wasn't intimidated by pressure, and most importantly, **wasn't afraid of failure**.

In many ways, he represented the direction Rajasthan Royals wanted to move towards—a **younger, bolder and more attacking brand of cricket**.

The message from the franchise was clear.

This wasn't a transitional captaincy.

**This was the beginning of the Riyan Parag era.**

Despite all the planning, trades and auction moves, **Rajasthan Royals entered IPL 2026 with several unanswered questions**.

Was **Vaibhav Sooryavanshi's breakthrough season** a glimpse of a generational talent, or would opposition teams figure him out after a year of analysis and preparation?

Who would take ownership of the crucial **No. 3 position**? The vacancy left by Nitish Rana was one of the biggest tactical questions in the squad, and RR still hadn't found an obvious answer.

Could **Jofra Archer finally stay fit for an entire IPL campaign**? When available, Archer was one of the most dangerous fast bowlers in the world. But after years of injury setbacks, RR fans knew better than to take his availability for granted.

There were other questions too. Could **Riyan Parag handle the pressure of captaincy**? Would **Ravindra Jadeja solve the spin-bowling issues** that haunted the team in 2025? How would the side cope **without Sam Curran**?

On paper, **Rajasthan Royals looked stronger than they had a year earlier**. But **paper has never won matches**.

The answers would only come once the tournament began.
